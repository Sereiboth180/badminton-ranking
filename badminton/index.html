Kento Momota

Japan

Kento Momota dominated men's singles badminton from 2018 through 2021, holding the World No.1 ranking for over three years almost without interruption.

🏆 Achievements

  • World No. 1 for 121 total weeks (2018-2021)
  • World Championships gold medalist (2018, 2019)
  • Multiple BWF World Tour Super 1000 titles
✖ Close

Viktor Axelsen

Denmark

Viktor Axelsen is one of the greatest men's singles players in badminton history, known for his power and physicality.

🏆 Achievements

  • Olympic Games gold medalist (Tokyo 2020 and Paris 2024)
  • World No. 1 for 183 total weeks across multiple spells
  • World Championships gold medalist (multiple times)
  • Highest BWF ranking points ever recorded in men's singles (122,606)
✖ Close

Chou Tien-chen

Chinese Taipei

Chou Tien-chen is one of Chinese Taipei's most consistent men's singles players, known for his calm, calculated playing style and longevity at the top level.

🏆 Achievements

  • Career-high World No. 2
  • Asian Games medalist
  • Multiple BWF World Tour Super 500/750 titles
✖ Close

Anders Antonsen

Denmark

Anders Antonsen is Denmark's top men's singles player, known for his all-round game. He reached his career-high ranking of World No.2 in September 2022.

🏆 Achievements

  • Career-high World No. 2 (27 September 2022)
  • World Championships medalist
  • European Championships gold medalist
✖ Close

  • World Championships medalist
✖ Close
